## 2. Quick Reference — Top 5 Facts to Cite

1. ⚠ **Official CMON Zombicide DLC for Tabletop Simulator exists.** Title: "Tabletop Simulator – Zombicide". Launch: June 3, 2016. Price: $9.99 USD. Publisher: Berserk Games + Cool Mini or Not joint. Reception: "Mostly Positive" with ~110 reviews (~79% positive). Coverage: Season 1 core set ONLY; expansions are not in the official DLC.
   - Source: [Tabletop Simulator – Zombicide on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/468726/Tabletop_Simulator__Zombicide/)
2. ⚠ **The community-built scripted version is much larger than the official DLC.** Top community mod ("Zombicide With Expansions and Scripts (Revived)") — 46,249 subscribers, 5-star avg, last updated Dec 2021. **Requires the host to own the official DLC** as license-gate.
   - Source: [Steam Workshop: Zombicide With Expansions and Scripts (Revived)](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2066064039)
3. ⚠ **CMON has used TTS as a Kickstarter conversion channel.** Documented examples: Cyberpunk 2077: Gangs of Night City (CMON × CD Projekt Red, June 2022 KS, $886,783 / 6,345 backers); Cthulhu: Death May Die: Fear of the Unknown (free public Workshop demo with CMON's permission); Massive Darkness 2: Hellscape (community-built demo, CMON pointed backers to it on Facebook, KS raised $3.8M / 21,763 backers). Pattern: free Workshop demo with subset of content + "components subject to change" disclaimer = de-risking the pledge for backers.
4. ⚠ **CMON sold the Zombicide IP to Asmodee in June 2025** amid financial distress (revenue down 17% to ~$37.4M, crowdfunding revenue down 25%, $3M+ losses, Hong Kong stock delisting). CMON also sold Blood Rage, Rising Sun, Ankh, Arcadia Quest, and Starcadia Quest (to Tabletop Tycoon).
   - Sources: [Wargamer — CMON sells Zombicide to Asmodee (June 2025)](https://www.wargamer.com/zombicide/board-game-rights-sold-to-asmodee), [BoardGameWire — Asmodee buys Zombicide IP](https://boardgamewire.com/index.php/2025/06/10/asmodee-buys-zombicide-ip-from-struggling-cmon-as-first-move-in-new-acquisition-spree/)
5. ⚠ **TTS daily concurrent users ~6,000 (May 2026)**, peak 17,555 in Jan 2026, all-time peak 37K. **Steam comparison: ~30M daily concurrent.** TTS is a niche tabletop hobbyist channel, not a mass digital channel.
   - Source: [SteamCharts — Tabletop Simulator](https://steamcharts.com/app/286160)

## 3. Official vs Community Implementations

### 3a. Official CMON paid TTS DLC

- ⚠ **Tabletop Simulator – Zombicide**: launched June 3, 2016, $9.99 USD, joint publisher Berserk Games + CMON, "Mostly Positive" reception with ~110 reviews (~79% positive). Season 1 core set only.
  - Source: [Tabletop Simulator – Zombicide on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/468726/Tabletop_Simulator__Zombicide/)
- ⚠ **Tabletop Simulator – Blood Rage**: launched September 7, 2019, $7.99 USD. Another paid CMON DLC.
  - Source: [Tabletop Simulator – Blood Rage on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/610700/Tabletop_Simulator__Blood_Rage/)
- ⚠ **DLC ownership model**: only the host needs to own the DLC for all players in a session to access the assets. Standard TTS DLC pattern.

### 3b. Official-permitted free Workshop demos (Kickstarter conversion assets)

- ⚠ **Cthulhu: Death May Die: Fear of the Unknown**: free public Workshop module, posted with CMON's permission and trademark in association.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop: Cthulhu: Death May Die [COMPLETE]](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2118293819)
- ⚠ **Cyberpunk 2077: Gangs of Night City** (CMON × CD Projekt Red, June 2022): officially-permitted free Workshop demo, labelled "CMON Kickstarter Demo," published *during* the live Kickstarter campaign.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop: Cyberpunk 2077: Gangs of Night City [CMON Kickstarter Demo]](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2827229184)
- ⚠ **Massive Darkness 2: Hellscape demo**: community-built playable preview, CMON pointed Kickstarter backers to it on Facebook. Disclaimer: "components and rules are not final."
  - Source: [Steam Workshop: Massive Darkness: Hellscape Demo](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2172749335)

### 3c. Community Workshop mod ecosystem (Zombicide-specific)

- ⚠ **"Zombicide With Expansions and Scripts (Revived)"** by DerOkon & Fuffuf — **46,249 subscribers**, 5-star avg, last updated Dec 2021. Requires official DLC as license-gate.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2066064039)
- ⚠ **"Zombicide With Expansions and Scripts [V9.3]"** by Solundor / claytja / Xeno — 24,233 subscribers, posted Oct 2017.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1172277561)
- ⚠ **"Zombicide 2nd Edition + Exp"** by timewolf — 17,910 subscribers, 4-star, last updated Feb 2023.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2480231285)
- ⚠ **Combined top-3 Zombicide community mods: 88,392+ subscriptions** (with overlap, but indicative scale)

### 3d. Community mods for other CMON properties (no paid DLC)

- ⚠ **Marvel United, Massive Darkness, Massive Darkness 2: Hellscape, and Rising Sun** do NOT have paid official DLC. They exist only as community Workshop mods.
  - Source: [Steam Workshop: Marvel United – Complete Scripted Edition](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2186402706)

### 3e. CMON's posture toward community mods

- ⚠ Tolerated. **No documented takedowns of Zombicide community mods.** Implicit posture: tolerance, with the official DLC required as the asset-licensing chokepoint (community mods inherit licensed assets via DLC ownership requirement).

## 4. Scope and Feature Set

### 4a. Official DLC scope

- ⚠ Essentially **digitised physical components** (boards, miniatures, cards, dice) + standard TTS physics. **No automation in the official DLC.**

### 4b. Community "Revived" mod scope (the version most players use)

Heavily LUA-scripted automation includes:
- ⚠ Mission setup and pre-building
- ⚠ Zombie spawning across up to 15 zones
- ⚠ Deck assembly
- ⚠ XP tracking
- ⚠ Draw/discard mechanics
- ⚠ Noise token removal
- ⚠ Zombie counting

(All from the same Workshop description: [Zombicide Revived](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2066064039))

### 4c. The 2nd Edition mod

- ⚠ Adds auto-setup for missions on top of base 2nd Edition components.
  - Source: [Zombicide 2nd Edition + Exp Workshop](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2480231285)

### 4d. What's NOT automated, even in the heaviest community mods

- ⚠ Rule arbitration
- ⚠ Line-of-sight judgment
- ⚠ Edge-case interpretations
- ⚠ **Players still need the rulebook open.**

**Strategic positioning of TTS Zombicide**: firmly companion/sandbox, not a standalone playable digital game. "The physical box with rules you must already know, brought online so distant friends can play."

## 5. Business Model and Physical-Sales Relationship

### 5a. Pricing

- ⚠ **Paid CMON DLC**: $7.99–$9.99 (Zombicide $9.99, Blood Rage $7.99)
- ⚠ **Demo Workshop modules**: free (CDMD, Cyberpunk 2077, MD2 demo)

### 5b. CMON's Kickstarter funnel pattern (most strategically important)

- ⚠ **Cyberpunk 2077: Gangs of Night City** (June 2022 Kickstarter): raised **$886,783 from 6,345 backers**. Workshop demo was published during the campaign.
- ⚠ **Cthulhu: Death May Die: Fear of the Unknown**: free Workshop demo released during live campaign.
- ⚠ **Massive Darkness 2: Hellscape**: community-built demo, CMON pointed backers to it on Facebook. Kickstarter raised **$3.8M from 21,763 backers**.

**Documented pattern**: free Workshop demo, subset of content, clearly marked as preview, timed to the live campaign. Used to convert wishlist-stage backers into pledges.

### 5c. Marketing/conversion claims — what's confirmed vs unconfirmed

- ⚠ **Active use of TTS as Kickstarter demo channel: CONFIRMED via Workshop labels and module disclaimers.**
- ✗ **Quantified TTS → physical conversion metrics: NOT PUBLIC.** No CMON exec interview, GDC talk, blog post, or press release found that quantifies the conversion rate.
- ✗ **Discount codes, bundle deals, "buy physical, get TTS" promotions: NONE FOUND** in the agent search.

**Strategic implication**: The Kickstarter demo pattern is observable from artifacts (Workshop pages, module labels, disclaimers), but the actual conversion economics are not disclosed. Treat as a strategically interesting precedent, not a quantified ROI claim.

## 8. Technical and Learning-Curve Notes

### 8a. TTS development environment

- ⚠ **LUA 5.2** scripting language
- ⚠ Atom and VS Code plugins
- ⚠ Official API at [api.tabletopsimulator.com](https://api.tabletopsimulator.com/)

### 8b. Build effort

- ⚠ Berserk Games' own blog: module development "can be a time sinkhole" spanning "hours, days, weeks, months and even years to perfect your game in terms of graphics and scripting."
  - Source: [Berserk Games TTS Scripting Tutorial](https://blog.tabletopsimulator.com/blog/scripting-on-tabletop-simulator-series-part-1)
- ⚠ The "Revived" Zombicide mod has been actively maintained for **4+ years across multiple authors**.
- ✗ **No published industry-standard cost/hours estimate** for "build a TTS module for a known tabletop game." Build effort scales entirely with automation depth.

### 8c. Effort estimate ranges

- A static digitised-components module: **a few weeks of asset prep** (unverified, rough estimate from agent inference based on the Workshop ecosystem)
- A heavily-scripted module like the Zombicide Revived mod: **years of cumulative community engineering**

## 9. Strategic Implications for Modiphius / 5PFH

### 9a. TTS validates Chris's framing

- The research **validates** Chris's framing ("more a wargame / miniatures sandbox; not designed to run a game like Five Parsecs to play actual battles"), it does not contradict it.
- Even at its most heavily LUA-scripted, TTS Zombicide is a digitised-physical-components experience that requires the rulebook open and human rule arbitration.
- **TTS = "play the physical game with distant friends"** vs **5PFH Steam app = "run the solo campaign loop on its own"** — different shape, different audience.
- Audience scale: TTS daily concurrent ~6K vs Steam's ~30M daily.
- **Reinforcing, not competing.** T1 (companion-not-port) and T2 (establishing the category) hold.

### 9b. The Kickstarter-demo pattern is potentially adoptable for Modiphius

- Free, time-limited TTS module released alongside a future Five Parsecs Kickstarter or expansion launch could function as an upper-funnel asset.
- Development cost: weeks-not-months for a static module.
- **But it's a marketing channel, not a core product.** TTS reach is for the dedicated tabletop crowd, not the digital-first Steam audience the 5x platform is aimed at.
- **Doesn't change the Steam-first plan.** Phase 2 supplemental experiment at most.

### 9c. Whether to mention TTS in the deal-counter email

- **Don't fold this into the deal-counter email** — it'll dilute focus on `$X` / `$Y` / framework language.
- Mention it in the May 18 conversation instead, or as a separate short note.

### 9d. Why CMON (not Modiphius) is the right precedent

- CMON's Zombicide / Marvel United / CDMD / Massive Darkness portfolio is more directly comparable to Modiphius's Five Parsecs / Star Trek / Fallout portfolio than any other tabletop publisher's TTS strategy.
- Both are mid-tier tabletop publishers with substantial Kickstarter dependency and licensed-IP exposure.
- The Asmodee acquisition adds urgency: Asmodee now owns Zombicide and may iterate on the digital strategy in unpredictable ways. Worth tracking.
