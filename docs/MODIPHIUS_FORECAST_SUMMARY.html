  • T4 — Active digital→physical conversion strategy. The app drives Steam users toward physical book purchase via in-app mechanisms — see Section E.

Break-even threshold runs from $100K Steam net (at $30K + 20%) up to $180K (at $45K + 25%) — above that, contractor structure costs Modiphius less than pure 50/50.
  • Frame B — Post-launch retainer (recommended pitch lead): $3–5K/mo retainer for 6–12 months post-EA + pure 50/50 rev share.   • Frame C — Hybrid: smaller intro fee ($15–20K) + 50/50 + post-launch retainer ($3K/mo × 6mo = $18K). Total Modiphius upfront commitment $33–38K. Like Frame B, this is pure additional cost (rev share stays 50/50) — split across two timeline phases. Combines platform investment up-front with post-launch availability.

Pure 50/50 with no contractor structure is the fallback baseline. I'd like a directional read on which frame resonates so I can refine.

E. Active Digital→Physical Strategy (T4)

Five in-app mechanisms designed to drive Steam users toward physical book purchases. Designed and prototyped during Phase B closed alpha:

  1. Steam-buyer discount code for the Core Rulebook (15–20% placeholder, awaiting your input on sizing)
  2. "Get the Physical Edition" CTA — persistent low-friction link to Modiphius store
  3. Bundled-PDF reminder — surfaces "physical includes free PDF" message at decision moments
  4. Tier-locked physical pre-order incentives for new physical expansions
  5. Modiphius newsletter capture — opt-in subscription flow

Modiphius keeps 100% of physical margin (no 50/50 applies to physical). Every app→book conversion is net additive to Modiphius's physical revenue at no incremental cost. Coordination items: discount sizing, redemption mechanism, co-branded landing page, newsletter API endpoint.
