# Modiphius Entertainment — Steam / Digital Platform Research

**Date:** March 11, 2026
**Purpose:** Pre-pitch intelligence on Modiphius's digital footprint, relevant to the Five Parsecs Campaign Manager partnership approach.

---

## Finding: Modiphius Has No Steam Presence

Modiphius Entertainment has **zero presence on Steam**. They are a pure physical/PDF tabletop publisher and have never developed or published a video game. Their digital distribution is limited to:

- Their own webstore (modiphius.net) — primarily PDF rulebooks and supplements
- DriveThruRPG — PDF distribution and their 2d20 World Builders community content program

They have no Steam developer account, no digital game infrastructure, and no internal expertise in game platform pipelines (patch management, store page management, Steam review process, etc.).

---

## What This Means for the Pitch

### You Bring Infrastructure They Don't Have

Modiphius has no frame of reference for managing an app on the App Store or Google Play. That operational knowledge — build pipelines, platform submission, update cadence, rating compliance — is something you bring to the table entirely. This shifts the value proposition: you're not asking them to take a risk on an untested developer; you're offering to handle a distribution channel they've never touched.

### Steam Is the Wrong Platform Anyway

The Five Parsecs Campaign Manager is a **tabletop companion app**, not a PC game. The natural home is mobile (iOS and Android), where the app lives on a phone next to the dice on the table during a session. Steam is a PC gaming destination; players don't fire up Steam to look up a post-battle injury table mid-session. The App Store and Google Play are the right conversation.

### The Maloric Digital Model Is the Blueprint

Jamie Morris's Fallout: Wasteland Warfare companion app (Maloric Digital) was distributed through Apple and Google under Modiphius branding — not Steam. That precedent is directly applicable. Morris approached Chris Birch cold with a 40-hour prototype, demoed at Dragonmeet, and was offered official status on the spot. The Five Parsecs Campaign Manager is dramatically further along than that prototype was.

### Their Only Digital Adjacency Is DriveThruRPG

Modiphius is comfortable with PDF community content programs (their 2d20 World Builders partnership with DriveThruRPG demonstrates this), but an app on the App Store and Play Store would be genuinely new territory for them. This is both the reason they need a partner for it **and** the reason the pitch conversation will require some education — Chris Birch will need the model explained in concrete terms (revenue flow, platform fees, update process) before he can evaluate it confidently.

---

## Pitch Framing Notes

Do **not** mention Steam in the pitch. Frame the opportunity as:

> "The official 5X platform — a companion app that lives on the player's phone at the table, distributed through Apple and Google the same way Jamie Morris handles the Fallout companion."

The Maloric Digital reference does two things: it proves Modiphius has already approved this model once, and it gives Chris a mental framework for what the deal structure looks like without requiring him to imagine it from scratch.
