# Chapter 5: Travel and World Phases

> **Quick Start** (for tabletop veterans)
> - Travel: Stay (free) or move to new world (5 credits ship / 1 credit per crew commercial)
> - Travel events rolled on D100 table; invasion escape needs 8+ on 2d6
> - World Phase has 6 sub-steps: Upkeep, Crew Tasks, Job Offers, Assign Equipment, Rumors, Mission Prep
> - Upkeep is 1 credit per crew per turn + ship maintenance
> - Crew tasks are the main strategic lever between battles

## Overview

The Travel and World phases make up the bulk of your between-battle decision-making. Travel determines where you operate, while the World Phase covers everything from paying the bills to choosing your next fight.

## The Travel Phase

### Stay or Go?

At the start of each turn's travel phase, you decide:

- **Stay** on your current world — No cost, no travel events. You continue operating where you are.
- **Travel** to a new world — Costs credits and may trigger a travel event, but gives you access to different missions, patrons, and marketplace inventories.

### Travel Costs

| Method | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Starship | 5 credits | Using your own ship |
| Commercial passage | 1 credit per crew member | No ship required |

If your ship is damaged or you can't afford starship travel, commercial passage is the fallback.

### Travel Events

When you travel, the app rolls a D100 travel event. Most trips are uneventful, but sometimes you'll encounter:

- **Asteroid fields** — Risk of ship damage
- **Pirates** — Possible combat encounter
- **Navigation trouble** — Delays or detours
- **Discovered wrecks** — Salvage opportunities
- **Drive troubles** — Mechanical issues requiring repair
- **Distress signals** — Help others for reputation, or ignore them

Each event has specific consequences described on screen. Some require dice rolls; others present choices.

### Invasion Escape

If your current world is under invasion (triggered by a galactic war check in a previous turn), you must decide whether to flee:

- Roll **2d6** — need **8+** to escape successfully
- **Success**: You travel to a safe world
- **Failure**: You may lose crew members or equipment before escaping

### Arriving at a New World

When you arrive at a new world, the app generates:

- World traits (danger level, tech level, government)
- Available patrons and job offers
- Local marketplace inventory
- Any special conditions or events

## The World Phase

The World Phase contains six sub-steps that you work through in order.

### Sub-Step 1: Upkeep

Pay your crew and ship maintenance costs before doing anything else.

**Costs per turn:**
- **Crew upkeep**: 1 credit per crew member
- **Ship maintenance**: Varies by ship type (typically 1-5 credits)
- **Equipment repairs**: Variable, if any gear is damaged

If you can't afford upkeep, the app handles the consequences per the rules — crew morale drops, and you may need to make difficult choices about what to prioritize.

### Sub-Step 2: Crew Tasks

Assign each available crew member a task for the turn. This is one of the most important strategic decisions you make between battles.

Available tasks:

| Task | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Guard the ship** | Prevents theft events, protects cargo |
| **Repair the ship** | Restores hull points (if damaged) |
| **Scout for enemies** | Reveals information about upcoming battle |
| **Scout for jobs** | Discovers additional mission opportunities |
| **Explore the underworld** | Finds black market deals, risks encounters |
| **Seek patrons** | Increases chance of patron job offers |
| **Train** | Gains training points toward skill improvements |
| **Rest / Recover** | Speeds up injury recovery |

**Decision Guidance:**
- Always assign someone to **guard the ship** if you have valuable cargo
- **Training** is a long-term investment — characters need multiple training turns to gain skills
- **Seeking patrons** is valuable early in a campaign when you have few patron contacts
- **Resting** crew members with injuries recover faster than those on active duty

Crew members with active injuries have limited task options — they can only rest or guard.

### Sub-Step 3: Job Offers

Review available missions from your patron contacts and the open job board.

Each job offer shows:
- **Patron name** (if from a patron)
- **Mission type** (standard, stealth, street fight, salvage, etc.)
- **Difficulty rating** — Affects enemy count and danger
- **Reward** — Credits and potentially other bonuses
- **Special conditions** — Any unusual rules or requirements

You don't have to accept any job. You can also encounter missions through random events or quest chains.

### Sub-Step 4: Assign Equipment

Redistribute weapons, armor, and items among your crew before heading into battle.

The equipment panel shows:
- Each crew member's current loadout
- Items in your ship stash (unequipped gear)
- Equipment stats for comparison

Make sure everyone going into battle has appropriate gear. See {{chapter:10}} for details on equipment management.

### Sub-Step 5: Resolve Rumors

If your crew has gathered any rumors (from previous turns, crew tasks, or events), you can follow up on them here.

Rumors may lead to:
- **Quest chains** — Multi-turn story arcs with increasing rewards
- **Special missions** — Unique battle scenarios
- **Information** — Intelligence about enemies or opportunities
- **Nothing** — Sometimes a rumor is just a rumor

### Sub-Step 6: Mission Prep (Choose Your Battle)

The final sub-step is selecting which mission to undertake:

- Review all available missions (patron jobs, quest missions, random encounters)
- Check the difficulty and reward for each
- Select your mission — or choose to **skip battle this turn**

Skipping battle is sometimes wise (e.g., if your crew is badly injured), but you still pay upkeep costs and miss out on XP and loot.

Once you select a mission, the app advances to the Battle Setup phase.

## World Phase Tips

- **Manage your economy.** Upkeep costs add up fast with a large crew. Keep a credit buffer.
- **Diversify crew tasks.** Don't send everyone to do the same thing — spread tasks for maximum benefit.
- **Build patron relationships.** Patron jobs are more reliable and often better-paying than random missions.
- **Don't ignore rumors.** Quest chains can lead to some of the best rewards in the game.
- **Consider skipping battle.** If your crew is wounded and low on resources, a turn of rest and recovery can be more valuable than a risky fight.
