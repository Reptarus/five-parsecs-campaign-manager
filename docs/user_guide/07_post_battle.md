# Chapter 7: Post-Battle Sequence

> **Quick Start** (for tabletop veterans)
> - 14 sequential steps resolve automatically with your input at key points
> - Pay comes from base mission reward + danger multiplier + difficulty bonus
> - Hold field (3+ kills) grants bonus loot rolls
> - Injuries use D100 table; recovery is 1d6 turns
> - XP awarded from 7 sources: participation, victory bonus, first kill, survival after injury, etc.

## Overview

After every battle, the app walks you through a 14-step post-battle sequence. Most steps resolve automatically based on your battle results, but several require decisions from you. This is where your crew gets paid, finds loot, treats injuries, gains experience, and deals with campaign events.

The Post-Battle Sequence runs in a fixed order. You can't skip steps, but some resolve instantly if they don't apply to your situation.

## The 14 Steps

### Step 1: Resolve Rival Status

If you fought a rival crew, the app determines what happens to the rivalry:

- **Rival eliminated**: Rivalry ends, reputation gained
- **Rival survives**: Rivalry continues, rival may escalate next encounter
- A D6 roll determines the outcome

### Step 2: Resolve Patron Status

If this was a patron mission:

- **Mission success**: Patron relationship improves, future jobs may be better
- **Mission failure**: Patron disappointment, relationship may suffer

### Step 3: Determine Quest Progress

If this battle was part of a quest chain:

- Roll D6 to determine quest advancement
- Quest may progress to next stage, stay at current stage, or complete
- Completed quests grant bonus rewards

### Step 4: Get Paid

Your payment is calculated:

| Component | Typical Value |
|-----------|--------------|
| Base mission reward | 50-200 credits |
| Danger multiplier | x1.0 to x2.0 |
| Difficulty bonus | +10 to +100 credits |
| Patron bonus | Variable |

The app shows a detailed breakdown of your earnings.

### Step 5: Battlefield Finds

Search the battlefield for useful items:

- Roll to determine what you find
- Results range from nothing to valuable equipment or data
- **Hold Field bonus**: If you held the field (eliminated 3+ enemies), you get extra search rolls

### Step 6: Check for Invasion

The app checks whether a galactic war event triggers on your current world:

- Roll **2D6** — on **9+**, an invasion is announced
- If invaded, you'll need to decide whether to flee or fight in future turns
- This is a campaign-level event that affects the galaxy map

### Step 7: Gather Loot

Enemy-specific equipment drops based on what they were carrying:

- **Victory**: 0-2 items per battle
- **Defeat**: 0-1 items per battle
- **Hold Field bonus**: Additional roll
- Items include weapons, armor, gear, implants, credits, and crafting materials

Loot is added to your ship stash. You'll distribute it during the next World Phase.

### Step 8: Determine Injuries

For each crew member who was wounded during battle:

- Roll on the **D100 injury table**
- Results range from minor bruises to serious permanent injuries:

| Roll Range | Severity | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| 01-20 | Minor | Flesh wound — 1 turn recovery |
| 21-50 | Moderate | Broken bone — 2-3 turns recovery |
| 51-80 | Serious | Internal injury — 3-5 turns recovery |
| 81-95 | Critical | Permanent stat reduction |
| 96-100 | Fatal | Character death |

Recovery time is **1d6 turns** for most injuries. During recovery, crew members can only rest or guard the ship.

**Stars of Story** protection: Characters with Stars of Story points can avoid the worst outcomes by spending a point to reroll.

### Step 9: Experience and Upgrades

XP is awarded from up to 7 sources:

| Source | XP |
|--------|-----|
| Battle participation | 1 |
| Victory bonus | +2 |
| Defeat (survival) | +1 |
| First kill | +1 |
| Survived injury | +1 |
| Completed objective | +1 |
| Special achievement | Variable |

**Bots** do not gain XP — they use credits for upgrades instead.

XP accumulates and can be spent during the Advancement Phase. See {{chapter:08}}.

### Step 10: Advanced Training

If any crew member has accumulated training points (from the Train crew task), they may unlock a new skill:

- Training requires multiple turns of the Train task
- Available skills: Pilot, Mechanic, Medical, Merchant, Security, Broker, Bot Tech
- Training costs 15-20 credits per skill
- **Soulless characters cannot train skills** (special species rule)

### Step 11: Purchase Items

Quick access to the marketplace for immediate purchases:

- Available items depend on world tech level
- This is a convenience step — you can also trade during the Trading Phase
- Useful for immediately replacing destroyed equipment

### Step 12: Campaign Event

A D100 roll triggers a campaign-level narrative event:

- 100+ possible events from the core rulebook
- Events can be beneficial (found a cache, made a friend) or harmful (ambush, equipment failure)
- Some events start new quest chains or introduce patrons/rivals
- The event description and consequences are shown on screen

### Step 13: Character Event

Each crew member may experience a personal event:

- Development opportunities: gain contacts, learn secrets
- Complications: addictions, romantic entanglements, personal rivalries
- These events add narrative depth and sometimes mechanical effects

### Step 14: Galactic War Check

The final step tracks the state of the galaxy:

- Check if any planets have been invaded
- Update invasion status for worlds you've visited
- Track the overall galactic conflict state

This is mostly bookkeeping — the app handles it automatically.

## Post-Battle Tips

- **Prioritize injury treatment.** Badly wounded crew members who don't rest may suffer complications.
- **Don't spend all your credits.** Keep a reserve for upkeep costs next turn.
- **Check loot carefully.** Implants found as loot are automatically installed (up to 3 per character) — see {{chapter:09}}.
- **Read campaign events.** They often set up future opportunities or threats.
- **Track your XP.** Save it for expensive stat upgrades rather than spending small amounts frequently.
