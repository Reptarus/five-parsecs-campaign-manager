# Community Management Guide

## 1. Overview

This document outlines the strategy, tone, and guidelines for managing the player community for Five Parsecs Campaign Manager. The goal is to foster a positive, welcoming, and engaged community that feels connected to the game and its development.

## 2. Core Principles & Tone

-   **Be Responsive & Present**: Acknowledge feedback and questions in a timely manner. Even a simple "Thanks for the feedback, we're looking into it" is better than silence.
-   **Be Transparent**: Share what you can, when you can. Be open about the development process, what you're working on, and what the future holds. Use the `COMPENDIUM_EXPANSION_ANALYSIS.md` as a basis for roadmap discussions.
-   **Be Positive & Professional**: Maintain a friendly, professional, and encouraging tone. Avoid arguments and defensive language. Always thank players for their time and passion.
-   **Empower Players**: Encourage community-led activities like sharing campaign stories, posting painted miniatures (if applicable), or creating gameplay guides.

## 3. Official Community Platforms

-   **Primary Hub: Discord Server**
    -   **Purpose**: Real-time discussion, community building, feedback collection, and announcements.
    -   **Key Channels**:
        -   `#announcements`: For official news and patch notes.
        -   `#general-discussion`: For all things Five Parsecs.
        -   `#campaign-stories`: For players to share their narrative playthroughs.
        -   `#feedback-and-suggestions`: For structured feedback.
        -   `#bug-reports`: For guiding players to the in-game reporting tool.

-   **Secondary Hub: Steam Community Forums**
    -   **Purpose**: A public-facing forum for potential new players and those who prefer a traditional forum format.
    -   **Strategy**: Cross-post all major announcements and patch notes from Discord. Actively monitor for questions and bug reports.

## 4. Content & Engagement Cadence

A regular content schedule keeps the community engaged, especially between major updates.

-   **Weekly**: 
    -   **"Campaign Story of the Week"**: Highlight and share a cool story from the `#campaign-stories` channel.

-   **Bi-Weekly / Monthly**: 
    -   **Developer Update**: A short post about what has been worked on recently. Can be a high-level summary of bug fixes or progress on a new feature.

-   **Per Patch/Update**: 
    -   **Detailed Patch Notes**: Post on all platforms.
    -   **Future Plans**: Briefly mention what the next focus will be (e.g., "Now that v1.1 is out, our next focus is on tackling some of the medium-priority features from the Freelancer's Handbook...").

## 5. Moderation Guidelines

A clear set of rules is essential for a healthy community.

-   **The Rules**:
    1.  Be respectful to fellow players and the development team.
    2.  No hate speech, harassment, or personal attacks.
    3.  Keep discussions related to the game in the appropriate channels.
    4.  Do not share pirated content or links.
    5.  No excessive spamming or self-promotion.

-   **Moderation Actions**:
    1.  **Warning**: A public or private message for a first-time minor offense.
    2.  **Temporary Mute/Kick**: For repeated minor offenses or a more serious initial offense.
    3.  **Permanent Ban**: For severe offenses like hate speech or harassment.

## 6. Handling Difficult Situations

-   **Negative Feedback**: Thank the player for their honesty. Acknowledge their frustration. Do not get defensive. If they have a valid point, state that you will discuss it with the team.
-   **Feature Requests We Can't Do**: Be honest. Explain briefly why it's not feasible (e.g., "That's a really cool idea, but unfortunately it would require a major overhaul of the combat system that we aren't able to do right now.").
-   **Misinformation**: Politely and publicly correct any misinformation with the correct facts. Do not be accusatory.
